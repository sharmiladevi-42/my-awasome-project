Biography

Early Life: Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. Coming from a humble background, he was the son of a boat owner and learned the values of hard work and perseverance from a young age.

Education: Kalam pursued his education in aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology, where he developed a passion for flight and technology.

Career Milestones: He began his career as a scientist at ISRO, where he played a pivotal role in India’s first satellite launch vehicle, SLV-3. Later, he served as the Chief of India’s Defense Research and Development Organisation (DRDO), leading several crucial missile development projects.

Presidency: In 2002, Dr. Kalam became the 11th President of India, known for his approachable demeanor and dedication to youth empowerment. He served until 2007 and was beloved by people of all ages.

Achievements

  • Bharat Ratna (1997): The highest civilian award in India, awarded for his contributions to science and engineering.

  • Missile Man of India: Recognized for his significant contributions to India’s missile development program, including Agni and Prithvi missiles.

  • President of India (2002-2007)

  • Author: He wrote several books, including "Wings of Fire," an autobiography, and "Ignited Minds," which inspire the youth to dream big.

Inspiration

Philosophies: Dr. Kalam believed in the power of education, innovation, and hard work. He often emphasized the importance of dreaming and the role of youth in nation-building.

Notable Quotes:

  • "You have to dream before your dreams can come true."
  • "If you want to shine like a sun, first burn like a sun."
